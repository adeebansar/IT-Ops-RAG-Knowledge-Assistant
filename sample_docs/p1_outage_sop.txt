P1 OUTAGE SOP - IT OPERATIONS

Purpose:
This SOP defines how the IT Operations team responds to a Priority 1 outage.

Definition:
A P1 outage is a critical service disruption affecting business-critical systems, multiple users, customer-facing platforms, or revenue-impacting workflows.

Initial Response:
1. Acknowledge the incident within 5 minutes.
2. Confirm user impact, affected systems, start time, and current symptoms.
3. Assign an Incident Commander.
4. Open a bridge call if the outage affects multiple teams.
5. Notify Service Desk, Network Support, Application Support, and Business Stakeholders.

Escalation:
If the outage is not mitigated within 15 minutes, escalate to senior infrastructure support and the IT Operations Manager.
If customer-facing services are affected, notify leadership and customer support leads.

Communication:
Send status updates every 30 minutes until service is restored.
Each update should include impact, current action, owner, next update time, and known workaround.

Resolution:
After service restoration, validate with monitoring tools and affected users.
Create a post-incident review within 2 business days.

Post-Incident Review:
Document root cause, timeline, business impact, missed alerts, corrective actions, and prevention steps.
